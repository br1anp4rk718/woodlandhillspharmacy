Fusion Bone Binder

Fusion: Calcium Sulfate Bone Graft Binder

Fusion is a binder for creation of a bone putty for intraoral grafting and socket preservation for future implant placement.

Fusion is a calcium sulfate material that contains resorbable surgical grade plaster of paris with 10% carboxymethylcellulose (CMC). The calcium sulfate/CMC material is mixed with gentamicin 3% solution to form the bone graft binder.

Each Fusion graft set comes packaged with two luer-lock syringes and coupling mechanism provided for mixing purposes. After mixing, Fusion is combined with your preferred bone graft material and forms a putty-like composite that fills defects to predictably regenerate bone. Fusion is easily molded to conform to any osseous or periodontal defect.

Clinical Advantages: Reduces migration of bone graft particles Produces more vital bone than other graft materials Resorption corresponds to the natural rate of bone formation, creating space for vital bone regeneration Creates a stable structure in the presence of fluids Uniform particle size, form, and structure offer the clinician predictable results Excelent antibiotic release characteristics
